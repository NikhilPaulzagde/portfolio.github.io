AtliQ Hospitality

Goal: The goal is to establish a data analytics team at AtliQ Grands, a long-standing hotel company, to use "Business and Data Intelligence" and regain lost customers and revenue due to competition and past management decisions.

Tools: Power BI , Excel


1. Identify Challenges: Understand the problems AtliQ Grands is facing, like losing customers and money due to competition and bad decisions.

2. Define Key Metrics: Work with the team to figure out what numbers are most important for making smart choices.

3. Design a Dashboard: Create a clear and easy-to-read dashboard that shows these key numbers.

4. Analyze Data: Look at a big set of data (13,500 records) carefully to make sure it's right for analysis.

5. Visualize Insights: Use a tool called Power BI to make graphs and charts that show how AtliQ Grands is doing.

6. Provide Insights: Go beyond the basic numbers to find important information and give it to AtliQ Grands to help them make better decisions.

7. Track Progress: Keep checking to see if the changes are helping AtliQ Grands get more customers and money, making sure they stay competitive.

Room Class Revenue and Average Ratings: The Elite room class generates the highest revenue, indicating that guests are willing to pay more for these premium accommodations. However, the Premium room class has a lower average rating compared to other classes, suggesting that while it generates revenue, there may be room for improvement in guest satisfaction.

Top Revenue Properties and Property Average Ratings: Atliq Exotica, Atliq Palace, and Atliq City are the top three revenue-generating properties. These properties are popular and attract a high number of guests, contributing to their revenue. Atiq Blu has a high average rating, indicating that it is well-received by guests, while Atliq Seasons has a lower rating. Improvements in guest satisfaction at Atliq Seasons could enhance its revenue.

City-wise Revenue and City Capacity: Mumbai is the top revenue-generating city, followed by Bangalore, while Delhi generates the least revenue. Factors like demand, pricing, and the local economy influence these revenue variations. Mumbai has the highest capacity, followed by Hyderabad, suggesting that these cities have a greater demand for accommodations or offer properties with larger capacities.

Booking Platforms and Daily Bookings: Other booking platforms and MakeMyTrip are the top three revenue-generating booking platforms, highlighting the hotel's effective distribution strategy. Daily bookings remained consistent at around 4,500 per day for the first 30 days but saw a significant drop to 1,100 on the 31st day, possibly due to seasonality or external factors affecting demand.

Cancellation Rate and Room Category Bookings: A consistent cancellation rate of approximately 27% indicates a stable booking and cancellation pattern. Understanding the reasons behind cancellations can help improve revenue management. RT2 Room Category has the highest number of bookings, suggesting guest preferences for this category, possibly due to its features, pricing, or availability.

Codex Analysis

Goal: The goal is to analyze survey results from 10,000 respondents in 10 Indian cities, with the aim of providing actionable insights to drive brand awareness, market share growth, and product development for CodeX in the Indian market.

Tools: SQL, Power BI


1. Data Analysis: As a Data Analyst Intern at Meriye LLC in the Food & Beverage industry, I analyzed a dataset containing 10,000 records.

2. SQL Expertise: I employed SQL techniques, including Joins, Count, Group by, Order by, Case statements, and Common Table Expressions (CTE) to extract and transform the data.

3. Data Visualization: Using Power BI, I visualized the data and created key performance indicators (KPIs) with DAX.

4. Decision-Making: My work empowered the marketing team with the ability to make data-driven decisions. The insights and visualizations I provided significantly improved our understanding of customer behavior and preferences within the Food & Beverage industry.

Demoghraphic: 60% of respondents (6,038 out of 10,000) are male, indicating a male preference for energy drinks. Over 50% of respondents are in the Age Group 19-30, highlighting the popularity of energy drinks among young adults. When considering age groups from 15 to 30, the percentage rises to 70%, emphasizing the strong connection between energy drink consumption and the youth demographic.

Consumer Preference: Caffeine is the most expected ingredient in energy drinks, known for increasing attention and alertness. Vitamins are also highly preferred in energy drinks. Compact and portable cans are in high demand, followed by innovative bottle designs, indicating the importance of packaging choices to consumers.

Purchasing Behaviour: Supermarkets are the most common choice for consumers to buy energy drinks. Energy drinks are popular for activities such as sports/exercise and studying/working late. The data indicates that young people are the primary consumers of these drinks. 43% of consumers prefer to purchase a product when it falls within the price range of 50-99. 40% of consumers do not expect changes in packaging, while 39% are open to trying limited edition packaging, suggesting a balance between stability and novelty in packaging preferences.

Competition Analysis: Cola Coka is the market leader, with a higher number of respondents favoring it, followed by Bepsi. Brand reputation is the top reason for consumers choosing their preferred brand, highlighting the significance of brand trust and recognition in the market.

Brand Penetration: Marketing efforts should focus on improving the positive response to the CodeX brand. Out of 980 respondents, 455 are aware of the CodeX brand and their ratings contribute to the product's taste. The taste rating of 3.3 aligns with the industry average.

Bank Churn Analysis

Goal: The goal of Bank Churn Analysis is to reduce the number of customers leaving the bank and improve overall customer satisfaction and the bank's performance.

Tools: SQL, Power BI


1.Get Data from SQL: Start by collecting information from the bank's database, like details about customers and their transactions.

2.Clean and Transform Data: Use a special tool to make sure the data is neat and organized. Fix any mistakes and make it ready for analysis.

3.Create Important Measures with DAX: With a tool called DAX, make important numbers that help us understand how the bank is doing. These numbers are like signs that show if customers are happy and if the bank is making money

4.Visualize Data: Use graphs and charts to show the data in a way that's easy to understand. Look at these visuals to find out how customers behave and what trends we can see.

1. Membership Status: - Out of 10,000 customers, 5,151 are still active, and 4,849 are not using the service anymore.

2. Credit Card Use: - 7,055 people have credit cards, and 2,945 don't.

3. Customer Leaving: Over the years, a total of 2,037 customers left. In different years, some customers left, but the numbers changed.

4. Gender Matters: - More women (1,139) left compared to men (898).

5. Credit Type Impact: - People with "fair" credit left the most, followed by those with "poor" and "good" credit. People with "excellent" credit left the least.

6. Credit Card Influence: - More customers with credit cards left than those without.
